Company Overview & History

Overview

Steel Trust Corporation is one of the largest importers and wholesalers of a wide range of structural steel products in the Philippines, covering a vast clientele from various industries and handling numerous projects nationwide.

History

The company started its operations way back in 1955 as a humble hardware store along Juan Luna St. in Tondo, Manila. Throughout the years, it has developed its strength in construction materials trading and has grown into one of the major players in the industry. It was in the 1980's when the company decided to concentrate its business efforts on steel trading.

Clientelle

We serve all grovernment, private and multinational sectors involved in: construction, shipping, fishing, sugar, oil, mining, automotive, power, communications, fabrication, and manufacturing.

Supplier

We regularly import from major steel mills in Asia (China, Taiwan, Japan, Korea, and Thailand) and Russia.

Management & Staff

We have a dynamic and dedicated team that pledges its commitment to continously provide you with the same dependable service, uncomprimising quality, and most reasonable prices that we have gained reputation for in the past half-century.

Our Mission

To continously adapt to the emerging needs of the Philippine market, by providing our costumers with highly competitive prices, top quality steel products, and the most efficient customer service.

Our Vision

To become the leading and most reliable steel supplier in the country, and in the process, becoming the preferred one-stop shop for a full-range of steel products and services.

Giving Back

Concern for the economic well-being of its community has been one of the company's thrusts since its relocation in the area in 1977. As a way of giving back, a portion of the company's profit is being channeled back through the following regular projects in Barangay Bungad, Quezon City:

  1. College scholarship grants for graduating students of Judge Juan Luna High School
  2. Constant improvement of facilities in Bungad Elemtary School
  3. Annual medical and dental missions
  4. "Project Merry Christmas" for 500 families located in the barangay
  5. Co-funding and constant support for the Sto.Cristo Foundation livelihood projects
